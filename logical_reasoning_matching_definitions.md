### Q1.

**Definition:** A "Valid Contract" is an agreement that is legally binding and enforceable in a court of law. It must involve an offer, acceptance, consideration, and legal capacity of the parties.

**Problem:** John agrees to sell his car to Sarah for $5,000. Sarah accepts the offer and pays the amount. Both are adults of sound mind. Is this a Valid Contract?

A) Yes, because it meets the criteria of offer, acceptance, consideration, and capacity.
B) No, because it was not written on a specific legal form.
C) Yes, but only if Sarah has a driver's license.
D) No, because the car might have hidden defects.

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:**
The situation fulfills all the requirements mentioned in the definition: offer (selling car), acceptance (Sarah accepts), consideration ($5,000), and capacity (adults of sound mind).
