# Logical Reasoning - Statement and Conclusion MCQs

### Q1.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "In a one-day cricket match, the total runs made by a team were 200. Out of these, 160 runs were made by spinners."
**Conclusions:**
I. 80% of the team consists of spinners.
II. The opening batsmen were spinners.

A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** D
**Explanation:**
1. Conclusion I doesn't follow because scoring 80% of runs doesn't mean 80% of players are spinners. One or two spinners could score all these runs.
2. Conclusion II doesn't follow because there's no information about the batting order.
---

### Q2.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "The old order changed, yielding place to new."
**Conclusions:**
I. Change is the law of nature.
II. Discard old ideas because they are old.

A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:**
1. Conclusion I is a direct interpretation of the statement that everything changes eventually.
2. Conclusion II is an extreme and cynical interpretation not supported by the statement. The statement implies change happens, not that everything old is bad.

### Q3.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "Government has levied as much as 20 percent tax on the sale of petroleum products in the domestic market."
**Conclusions:**
I. People should boycott the purchase of petroleum products to show their protest.
II. The price of petroleum products would increase in the domestic market.

A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:**
1. Boycotting (I) is an action/suggestion, not a conclusion logical derived from the fact of a tax levy.
2. A high tax levy directly leads to an increase in price (II).

A) Only conclusion I follows

C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

### Q5.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "All the students in my class are bright. Manish is not bright."
**Conclusions:**
I. Some students are not bright.
II. Manish is not a student of my class.

A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Explanation:**

2. If Manish is not bright, and all students in the class are bright, Manish cannot be in that class.

### Q6.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "The use of non-conventional sources of energy can help in reducing pollution."
**Conclusions:**
I. Non-conventional sources of energy do not produce any pollution.
II. Over-dependence on conventional sources of energy is a cause of pollution.

A) Only conclusion I follows
B) Only conclusion II follows
C) Either I or II follows
D) Neither I nor II follows

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:**
1. "Reducing" doesn't mean "eliminating" (I).
2. If non-conventional reduces it, then current (presumably conventional) dominance is logically the cause.

### Q17.
**Problem:** 
**Statement:** "All those who have high blood pressure should avoid spicy food."
**Conclusions:**
I. Spicy food causes high blood pressure.
II. Some people have high blood pressure.

A) Neither I nor II follows
B) Only I follows
C) Only II follows
D) Both follow

**Answer:** A
**Explanation:**
1. Statement says spicy food should be "avoided," not that it's the "cause."
2. Statement starts with "All those who have...", which is a conditional, not a confirmation that such people exist in reality (though common sense says they do, logic looks at the statement). However, in many logic tests, "All A are B" implies A exists. But here, conclusion II is trivial and doesnt strictly "follow" as a deduction from the logic of blood pressure management.
