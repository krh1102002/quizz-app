# Logical Reasoning - Calendar MCQs

### Q1.
**Problem:** If today is Monday, what day will it be after 61 days?
A) Wednesday
B) Saturday
C) Tuesday
D) Thursday

**Answer:** B
**Explanation:**
1. Each day of the week repeats after every 7 days.
2. Divide 61 by 7: 61 = (7 x 8) + 5.
3. The remainder is 5.
4. Count 5 days ahead of Monday: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday.
So, the day will be Saturday.
---

### Q2.
**Problem:** If the 1st of October is a Sunday, then the 1st of November will be a:
A) Monday
B) Tuesday
C) Wednesday
D) Thursday

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:**
1. October has 31 days.
2. Remaining days in October = 31 - 1 = 30.
3. To reach 1st November, add 1 day: 30 + 1 = 31 days.
4. 31 divided by 7 gives a remainder of 3.
5. Sunday + 3 days = Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday.
---

### Q3.
**Problem:** Which of the following is a leap year?
A) 1900
B) 1800
C) 2000
D) 2100

**Answer:** C
**Explanation:**
1. A normal year is a leap year if it is divisible by 4.
2. A century year (ending in 00) is a leap year only if it is divisible by 400.
3. 2000 is divisible by 400, so it is a leap year. 1900, 1800, and 2100 are not.
---

### Q6.
**Problem:** The last day of a century cannot be:
A) Monday
B) Wednesday
C) Friday
D) Tuesday

**Answer:** D
**Explanation:**
1. 100 years have 5 odd days (Friday).
2. 200 years have 3 odd days (Wednesday).
3. 300 years have 1 odd day (Monday).
4. 400 years have 0 odd days (Sunday).
So, the last day of a century can be Friday, Wednesday, Monday, or Sunday. It cannot be Tuesday, Thursday, or Saturday.
